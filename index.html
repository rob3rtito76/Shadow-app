📖 Coach's cheat sheet — what to listen for
Schwa /ə/ Unstressed vowels collapse to "uh." "banana" → b'-NAN-uh. "to" → tə. "and" → n. Flap T Between vowels, T → quick D. "water" → wah-der. "got it" → gah-dit. "not at all" → nah-da-dall. Linking Consonant + vowel glues. "pick it up" → pi-ki-tup. "hang on" → han-gon. Reductions going to → gonna · want to → wanna · got to → gotta · did you → didja · what are you → whaddya · how is it → howzit. Stressed beats English is stress-timed: content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) get the BEAT; function words (the, of, to, a) compress between beats. Equal-syllable timing is the #1 non-native giveaway.
